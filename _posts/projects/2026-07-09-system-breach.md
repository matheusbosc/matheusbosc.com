## How the Team Came Together

Like most game jam teams, it started scattered. Two people found each other first, then a third joined. I was still looking for a team of my own, so I posted in the jam's Discord server — someone reached out, and I joined what would become our core group. From there, we filled the remaining gaps:

- An artist joined when we needed someone for visuals
- A composer/sound designer came on board not long after, for music and SFX

That was the full team — five people who'd never worked together before the jam, now building a game together.

## Finding the Idea

We didn't walk in with a concept — we brainstormed. Throwing out whatever ideas came to mind, seeing what stuck, until a few pieces clicked together into something we actually liked. That became our base idea. Once the jam's theme was revealed, we bent that idea to fit it, and System Breach started taking shape from there.

The result: **Unit 404**, an android breaking free from the corporate control of **Everwatch**, blending stealth and action gameplay with a branching moral ending — the player's choices shaping how the story closes.

## Why We Kept Going

We didn't win the jam. But by the time it ended, none of us wanted to stop:

- Every one of us had wanted to actually *ship* a game before — not just prototype and move on
- We genuinely liked what we'd built, both the game itself and how well we worked as a team

That was enough to turn a 48-hour project into **Trust The Process**, and System Breach into something we're still building toward release.

## Where It Stands Now

- **Platform:** PC, releasing on Steam
- **Engine:** Unity 6 LTS
- **Genre:** Pixel art stealth-action
- **Target:** March 2027 release planned

The team's grown since the jam too — it's now myself, another developer, Sam on 3D art, and Rea on sound. Along the way, I've also taken on store and capsule art myself, after our original 2D artist moved on to other things.

It didn't win a jam. But it's turned into a studio, a team, and a game we're actually proud to be finishing.
